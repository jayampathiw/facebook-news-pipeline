docs/sources-audit-france.mdExecutive Summary and Methodological FrameworkThe strategic realignment of the automated Facebook news aggregator, previously skewed toward international affairs and Parisian-centric macroeconomic reporting, necessitates a comprehensive integration of the Presse Quotidienne Régionale (PQR) and targeted lifestyle publications. An analysis of the existing ingestion pipeline—currently relying on national behemoths such as Le Monde, Le Figaro, BFM TV, and France 24—reveals a structural deficit in localized, provincial reporting. To rectify this imbalance and engage a broader demographic of French residents, the integration architecture must pivot to encompass regional powerhouses and niche cultural publishers.The French media landscape presents unique challenges for automated aggregation. Ownership is highly concentrated among a few dominant conglomerates, notably the Rossel Group, SIPA Ouest-France, the EBRA Group (Crédit Mutuel), and LVMH. This oligopolistic structure means that technical architectures, paywall strategies, and legal enforcement mechanisms are frequently shared across multiple regional mastheads. Furthermore, the transposition of the European Union Directive on Copyright in the Digital Single Market into French law has created a stringent regulatory environment concerning Neighbouring Rights (Droits Voisins). Organizations such as the Alliance de la presse d'information générale (APIG) vigorously defend the intellectual property of publishers against unauthorized scraping.Consequently, the extraction and hosting of visual assets present a profound legal vulnerability. While the native rendering of Open Graph (og:image) metadata by Facebook's proprietary crawler is legally protected under established link-sharing paradigms, the independent downloading, storage, and re-hosting of watermarked imagery from syndicates like AFP, SIPA, or Getty Images constitutes direct copyright infringement. The technical ingestion pipeline must therefore rigorously avoid raw image extraction, relying exclusively on passing URLs to the social graph interface.Additionally, French publishers are actively fortifying their perimeters against automated data extraction. The proliferation of generative artificial intelligence has prompted widespread modifications to robots.txt exclusion protocols. While benign news aggregators with transparent User-Agents (e.g., Googlebot-News) generally remain whitelisted to drive ad-subsidized traffic, aggressive scraping behavior or missing HTTP headers routinely trigger firewall interdictions.This document provides an exhaustive, multi-dimensional audit of nineteen candidate sources. Geographic / topic focus
Geographical dominance encompasses Brittany, Lower Normandy, and the Pays de la Loire. The editorial taxonomy is deeply rooted in local municipalities, extensive maritime and agricultural industry coverage, and provincial sports. Image rights
Intellectual property enforcement is rigorous. The publication's terms of use forbid the extraction and isolated commercial hosting of its visual assets.
