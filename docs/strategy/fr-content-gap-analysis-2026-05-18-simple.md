# France Content Strategy — Plain-English Version

**Page:** [facebook.com/FranceAujourdhui](https://www.facebook.com/FranceAujourdhui)
**Date:** 2026-05-18
**What we looked at:** All 248 articles we collected for France in the last 30 days. Of those: 23 were posted, 191 are still pending, 34 were blocked by our content rules, 0 are approved-but-unposted, 0 are rejected.
**What we compared it against:** 10 popular French Facebook posts from May 17–18, 2026, given in the brief.

> This is a simplified version of `fr-content-gap-analysis-2026-05-18.md`. Same facts, same ideas, easier words.

---

## Short Summary

Our page is publishing **serious world-news with calm, neutral writing and only still-image posts**. Meanwhile, popular French Facebook pages are winning with **local French drama, short videos (Reels), polls, and emotional stories**. Three big choices are causing this gap:

1. **We post too much foreign news.** More than 70% of our 23 posts are about the Middle East, Ukraine, or US-China relations. The popular posts are mostly about life inside France — farmers, knife attacks, French food, French politicians.
2. **We never use video.** All 23 of our posts are still images. 4 out of 10 popular posts are short videos (Reels).
3. **Our safety filters are blocking the most engaging stories.** Our pipeline blocked 34 articles because of rules around politics (24), weapons (7), legal cases (2), and drugs (1). These are exactly the topics that go viral in France — the Nantes shooting, the Sarkozy trial, Macron's decisions, RN politics, etc. We are protecting ourselves for paid ads, but we are losing on free reach.

**The single most important decision:** if we are not paying to boost posts, we should stop blocking these articles. We can still post them organically — we just can't run ads on them.

## 1. What the Market Has That We Don't

### 1.1 Story types we are missing

| Topic that's working in the market | What we posted in 30 days | Why this is a gap |
|---|---|---|
| **Rural and identity conflict** (farmer spraying caravans with manure) | 0 | Big gap — we have no "French drama" content at all |
| **Citizens doing patriotic acts** (man swapping a flag in Paris) | 0 | We write *about* nationalism abroad, never show it happening at home |
| **French street crime and self-defence debates** (BFMTV car-theft, Rennes knife attack) | 0 — and we blocked 7 stories like this | **We're doing this to ourselves** — our own filter is the cause |
| **Food, French institutions, "what a deal"** (€60 lobster buffet) | 0 | No "proud to be French" content at all |
| **Celebrities taking political sides** (Camille Cottin vs RN) | 0 — several similar items blocked or stuck pending | Blocked by our politics filter |
| **Yes/No polls about politics** (Macron poll) | 0 | We never ask quick-vote questions |
| **Tech-meets-France stories** (Elon Musk being investigated in France) | We posted Trump-Xi but not Musk-France | We do cover global news, but we miss the French angle |
| **2027 election signals** (Mélenchon rally) | 0 — Attal, Ruffin, Glucksmann all blocked | Blocked by our politics filter |
| **Local breaking news** (Rennes knife attack) | 0 | Blocked by our weapons filter (Nantes shooting was blocked) |
| **Throwback hero stories** (Gassama saved a child in 2018) | 0 | We only post breaking news. We never re-share classic feel-good stories |

**Where our news comes from.** Our 23 posts come from 7 big traditional outlets: BFM, France 24, Libération, Le Monde, Le Figaro, L'Obs, France Info. The popular posts also come from satire pages (*Le monde part en cacahuète*, 566K followers), opinion pages, food creators, and regional papers (*Ouest-France Rennes*). We have no satirical voice and no regional voice.

**How we write vs how they write.** Our headlines sound like a news desk: "Tensions frontalières Israël-Liban", "Diplomatie de façade sino-américaine". Their headlines hit harder: "A FARMER SPRAYS THEM WITH MANURE", "how do they make money?", "HAS MACRON WEAKENED THE STATE?"

### 1.2 Format gaps

- **Short videos (Reels): we have 0, market has 4 out of 10.** Reels are the only format Facebook gives a free boost to in 2026.
- **Polls / Yes-No questions: we have 0, market has 1.** That one poll got 184 comments on 1.6K likes — a very high comment rate (~12%).
- **Carousels (multi-image): neither of us uses these.** Could be an opportunity for explainers.
- **Raw "citizen-filmed" videos: we have 0, market has 1.** In a polarised news cycle, "raw and real" beats "polished".

### 1.3 Our captions are unfinished

When we look at the actual data for our 23 posted articles:

- The `intro` field is **empty in all 23**
- The `question` field is **empty in all 23**
- The `call-to-action (cta)` field is **empty in all 23**
- Only the image headline, the seed comment, and the hashtags are filled in.
- The exact same seed comment — `"💬 Et vous, qu'en pensez-vous ? Est-ce que cette nouvelle vous surprend ? Répondez en commentaire — on lit tout. 👇"` — appears **word-for-word on 8 of 23 posts**.

This is a code/pipeline problem, not a strategy problem. The system was built to fill these fields, but it isn't doing it. Together with the copy-pasted seed comment, the page feels robotic.

### 1.4 We are not asking for the right kind of reaction

Each popular post is designed to trigger **one specific easy reaction**:
- Anger split — pro/con in comments (Post 1)
- A vote — "67% NON, 33% OUI" (Post 7)
- Tag a friend — "let's go there" (Post 4)
- Identity signal — flag, banner (Posts 2, 8)
- A counter-story — "actually, immigrants can be heroes" (Post 10)

Our posts always ask the same kind of slow, analytical question ("Can mediation still lead to a real agreement?"). That's asking for an essay, not a quick reaction. So very few people comment.

**Patterns we see across all 10:**

- **Conflict beats information.** 7 out of 10 posts show a fight, not a fact. Ours show facts.
- **Local beats global.** Even the Musk post wins by adding a French angle. We do the opposite — we publish global stories without any French link.
- **Visual rule:** one strong subject, one short French sentence in capital letters, color used to set the mood (red = urgent, gold = good news). Some of our image headlines are in English ("Climate crisis acceleration threat", "Family acceptance, parental love journey", "Hisense Built-in Oven: Smart Deal") — **this is wrong on a French page**.
- **Time of day:** 7 of 10 popular posts went out between 07:00 and 12:35 Paris time (morning). We post whenever the cron job runs — no posting schedule.

## 3. What We Should Do (Sorted by Impact vs Effort)

### P0 — Fix these right now. No debate needed.

1. **Fix the caption code.** All 23 posted articles are missing intro, question, and CTA. The fields exist, the prompts exist, the dashboard expects them — but nothing is being written.
   - Who: whoever owns the pipeline.
   - Why it matters: doubles how much we can say per post and removes the templated feel.

2. **Force all image headlines to be in French.** About 6 of our 23 posts have English text on the page. Fix this in `claude.js` and in the edge function.
   - Cost: tiny. Just a prompt change.

3. **Stop reusing the same seed comment.** The "Et vous, qu'en pensez-vous ?" line shows up 8 times word for word. Either generate a unique one per article, or rotate between 10+ templates.
   - Why: readers notice repetition fast and stop trusting the page.

### P1 — Change what we publish (this week)

4. **Add a "Local France" track.** Right now we're 70%+ international. Target: 50% local France / 25% global news with a French angle / 25% lifestyle, culture, nostalgia.
   - How: add French domestic sources to `src/config/sources.js` — *Ouest-France*, *La Dépêche*, *20 Minutes*, *Le Parisien*. Tune the scoring logic in `src/utils/criticality.js` to favour stories with French place names.
   - Why: this is the biggest gap.

5. **Re-think the safety filters.** 31 of the 34 blocked articles were killed by the politics filter or the weapons filter — including all 2027 campaign stories, the Sarkozy trial, Macron decisions, the Nantes shooting. If we are not running paid ads, these filters are costing us our best content.
   - Decision needed: are we boosting posts? If not, change these gates from "block the post" to "mark as organic-only". Add a `boost_eligible: true/false` column instead of removing posts.
   - Why: this could unlock about 31 articles per month that we already generated.

6. **Start posting Reels (1–2 per week to begin with).** Pick story types that work in vertical video without filming anything ourselves: poll Reels (still images stacked with a voice question), explainer Reels (3 cards in 30 seconds), and re-packaged citizen videos with credit (when allowed). The flag-swap Reel was just raw user footage — no production needed.
   - Why: Reels still get priority on Facebook in 2026.

### P2 — Add new tools (next 2–4 weeks)

7. **Add a yes/no poll caption type.** Extend the `ai_caption` schema with an optional `poll: {question, optionA, optionB}` field. Show it as an image overlay (Anton font, two columns). Use it once per 5 posts max.

8. **Add an "Evergreen" pillar.** Once a week, re-share a classic positive French story (heroes, science wins, regional traditions, sports comebacks). Gassama worked in 2026 because it was *re-told*, not because it was new. Build a list of 30–50 evergreen stories and rotate them.

9. **Add a "Lifestyle / French Pride" pillar.** Food, regional places, value-shock items, "did you know this exists in France?" Low political risk, high share rate. Add sources like *Le Bonbon*, *TimeOut Paris*, *Demotivateur*.

10. **Schedule posting times.** Popular posts cluster between 07:00 and 12:35 Paris time. Right now we post every 30 minutes whenever cron runs. Change `services/facebook.js` so approved posts publish at 07:30, 12:00, and 19:00 Paris time. Also fix `posted_at` — it's NULL on all 23 posted rows.

### P3 — Bigger experiments (next month)

11. **Try satirical-style framing for 1 post a week.** Don't copy *Le monde part en cacahuète*, but learn from it: one shocking visual + a capital-letters tabloid headline + a clear source.

12. **Build a regional voice.** *Ouest-France Rennes* wins because the community trusts it. We could add city-tagged posts ("Lille aujourd'hui", "Marseille aujourd'hui") inside the same page so local stories reach local audiences.

## 4. Step-by-Step Implementation Guide

### 4.1 Topics to chase, in order of priority

1. Local crime and self-defence debates (unblock first — see Rec #5)
2. Domestic politics: 2027 candidates, Macron, RN, LFI (unblock first)
3. Rural and regional fights (farmers, water, gens du voyage)
4. French institutions + "shocking value" (buffets, train prices, hospital costs, top universities)
5. Celebrities taking political sides (actors, musicians, athletes)
6. Tech in France (Musk, Tesla, Starlink, Apple stores, foreign tech sued in French courts)
7. Throwback heroes (one a week, Gassama-style)
8. 2027 election signals (rallies, polls, programmes)
9. Regional safety news (in the style of *Ouest-France*)
10. Climate and extreme weather — but only when it has a French connection

### 4.2 Visual and formatting rules

| Element | Where we are | Where we should be |
|---|---|---|
| Language on the image | Mixed French / English | **French only — strict rule** |
| Headline style | Sentence case, neutral | **UPPERCASE, direct, max 12 words** |
| Use of color | Random | **Red = breaking, Blue = poll, Gold = positive, B&W = throwback** |
| Format mix per week | 100% still images | **60% still / 20% Reels / 15% poll / 5% carousel** |
| Watermark | Anton font, 70% opacity, bottom-right | Keep — already correct |
| Raw user footage | Never | Allowed in Reels when rights are clear |

### 4.3 Writing changes

- **Open with conflict, not analysis.** Instead of "The CIA director met with..." write "Washington tries a secret channel with Havana. Smart move or weakness?"
- **Always answer: "Why does this matter to someone living in France?"** Every international post needs one French-stake sentence.
- **Keep sentences short.** Popular captions are 1–2 short sentences. Our image headlines are already short — apply the same rule to `ai_caption.intro` once it's fixed.
- **End with a one-tap question, not an essay prompt.**
  - Old (today): "Can political dialogue still prevent worse tensions in Chad?"
  - New: "Dialogue, reform, or firmness? One word in the comments."
- **Use many seed-comment variants.** Build a pool of at least 10. Match them to story type and seriousness. Never reuse the same one within 7 days.

### 4.4 Content pillar plan

- **🔴 "France in debate"** — 3 times a week — domestic conflict stories with a yes/no question
- **🟢 "French pride"** — 2 times a week — food, institutions, throwback heroes, science wins
- **🟡 "The world from Paris"** — 2 times a week — global stories framed only for French readers
- **🔵 "Poll of the day"** — 1 a week — yes/no poll as a Reel or image
- **⚪ "Throwback"** — 1 a week — feel-good classic, posted on the weekend
- **📍 "My city today"** — 1 a week — regional story tagged with a city name

### 4.5 Posting time plan

- **Slot 1 — 07:00–08:00 Paris time:** breaking news and outrage stories (commuters scrolling, all-day debate). Posts 1, 4, 5 from the popular list hit here.
- **Slot 2 — 11:30–12:30 Paris time:** breaking-news graphics (lunchtime). Posts 3 and 9 hit here.
- **Slot 3 — 17:00–20:00 Paris time:** Reels and political content (after-work scroll). Posts 2, 8, 10 hit here.
- **Weekend mornings:** throwback / pride content. Post 10 (Gassama) hit Sunday evening.
- **Limit:** maximum 4 posts a day. Quality beats quantity on a new page.
